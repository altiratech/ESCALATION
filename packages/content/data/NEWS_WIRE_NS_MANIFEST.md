## Narrative Features

### Multiple Perspectives on Same Events

The news wire captures competing narratives around key moments:
- **Missile deployments:** U.S. defensive concern (Capital Standard) vs. Kaltor defensive posture (KSB) vs. technical analysis (Jane's) vs. market reaction (MarketPulse)
- **Trade friction:** U.S. retaliatory logic (Capital Standard) vs. Kaltor retaliation warning (KSB) vs. capital flight (Sovereign Risk)
- **Ceasefire negotiation:** Both sides claiming diplomatic flexibility while media scrutinizes terms

### Escalation Tracking

Tone and urgency shift across phases:
- **Opening:** Measured language, diplomatic language, routine announcements
- **Rising:** Growing tension, increasing alerts, fracturing consensus
- **Crisis:** Alarmist coverage, emergency mobilization, market panic
- **Climax:** Urgent military language, war declarations, existential stakes
- **Resolution:** Relief narratives, economic recovery, lingering instability

### Fog-of-War Elements

- Some articles report unconfirmed intelligence or OSINT guesses
- Pull quotes attribute claims to "officials on condition of anonymity"
- State media provides deliberately misleading spin
- Neutral outlets remain cautiously hedged in attribution
